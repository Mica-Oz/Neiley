Traffic Offenses

Defense Against Serious Traffic Violations & Vehicular Crimes

Traffic Offense Attorneys in Western Colorado

While many traffic violations can be handled by paying a fine, serious traffic offenses in Colorado carry criminal penalties including jail time, heavy fines, license suspension, and a permanent criminal record. From reckless driving on mountain highways to hit-and-run incidents and vehicular assault, these charges require experienced legal defense. At Neiley & Morris, we defend clients facing serious traffic charges throughout Telluride, Aspen, and Rifle.

Western Colorado's mountain roads present unique driving challenges—steep grades, sharp curves, winter weather conditions, and heavy tourist traffic create situations where even careful drivers can face criminal traffic charges. Our attorneys understand the local roads, weather conditions, and law enforcement practices that affect traffic cases in our region.

Reckless Driving

Reckless driving under C.R.S. § 42-4-1401 involves driving a vehicle in a manner that indicates a wanton or willful disregard for the safety of persons or property. This is a class 2 misdemeanor traffic offense carrying up to 90 days in jail, fines up to $300, and 8 points on your driving record. A second offense within 5 years is a class 1 misdemeanor traffic offense with up to 10 months in jail.

On Western Colorado's mountain highways and passes, reckless driving charges frequently arise from excessive speed on mountain roads and passes, aggressive driving in heavy tourist traffic, passing in no-passing zones on winding mountain roads, and winter driving incidents involving excessive speed for conditions. Prosecutors and judges in mountain communities take reckless driving seriously given the dangerous road conditions and the potential for catastrophic accidents on steep mountain roads.

Careless driving under C.R.S. § 42-4-1402 is a lesser charge involving driving without due regard for road conditions, traffic, and other circumstances. This is a class 2 misdemeanor traffic offense carrying up to 90 days in jail and 4 points on your record. Careless driving causing injury is a class 1 misdemeanor traffic offense with up to 364 days in jail and 6 points. Negotiating a reckless driving charge down to careless driving can significantly reduce penalties and long-term consequences.

Hit and Run

Leaving the scene of an accident is a serious criminal offense in Colorado. The severity of the charge depends on the nature of the accident. Leaving the scene of an accident involving only property damage is a class 2 misdemeanor traffic offense under C.R.S. § 42-4-1601, carrying up to 90 days in jail and 12 points on your license. Leaving the scene of an accident involving injury is a class 1 misdemeanor traffic offense carrying up to 364 days in jail. Leaving the scene of an accident involving death is a class 4 felony carrying 2 to 6 years in the Department of Corrections.

Colorado law requires drivers involved in an accident to stop immediately, provide their name, address, and registration to the other party, render reasonable assistance to injured persons, and report the accident to law enforcement when there are injuries or significant property damage. Failure to fulfill any of these duties can result in hit-and-run charges even if the accident itself was not the driver's fault.

Common defenses to hit-and-run charges include lack of knowledge that an accident occurred, leaving the scene to seek emergency assistance, inability to stop safely, and challenging identification evidence. Our attorneys thoroughly investigate the circumstances of each incident to build the strongest possible defense.

Driving Under Suspension or Revocation

Driving under suspension (DUS) or driving under revocation (DUR) is a serious traffic offense in Colorado. Driving under restraint under C.R.S. § 42-2-138 is a class 2 misdemeanor traffic offense for a first offense. If the underlying revocation was for a DUI-related offense, the charge becomes a class 1 misdemeanor traffic offense with mandatory jail time of 30 days to 364 days.

Many people are unaware their license has been suspended, which can happen due to unpaid traffic tickets or fines, failure to appear in court, failure to maintain insurance, accumulation of too many points, administrative actions related to DUI or DWAI, and out-of-state violations that trigger reciprocal suspensions. While lack of knowledge of the suspension can be a defense, it requires demonstrating that you had no reasonable way of knowing your license was suspended. Our attorneys help clients challenge DUS/DUR charges and work to restore driving privileges.

Vehicular Assault and Vehicular Homicide

Vehicular assault under C.R.S. § 18-3-205 occurs when a person operates a vehicle in a reckless manner and causes serious bodily injury to another. This is a class 5 felony carrying 1 to 3 years in the Department of Corrections. When the driver is under the influence of alcohol or drugs, vehicular assault becomes a class 4 felony carrying 2 to 6 years and is classified as a crime of violence with mandatory prison time.

Vehicular homicide under C.R.S. § 18-3-106 occurs when a person operates a vehicle in a reckless manner and causes the death of another person. This is a class 4 felony with 2 to 6 years. When committed while under the influence, vehicular homicide is a class 3 felony carrying 4 to 12 years and is classified as a crime of violence. These are among the most serious traffic-related charges in Colorado and require immediate, experienced legal defense.

Eluding a Police Officer

Eluding or attempting to elude a police officer under C.R.S. § 18-9-116.5 is a class 5 felony. If the eluding creates a substantial risk of bodily injury to another, it becomes a class 4 felony. Eluding charges often accompany other traffic offenses and can significantly increase the total penalties faced. Defenses may include challenging whether the police vehicle was properly marked, whether the officer properly signaled the driver to stop, and whether the defendant's actions constituted intentional eluding versus failure to notice the officer.

Commercial Driver Considerations

For holders of commercial driver's licenses (CDLs), traffic offenses carry enhanced consequences. A single serious traffic violation can result in CDL disqualification for 60 days. Two serious violations within 3 years trigger a 120-day disqualification. Certain offenses—including DUI, hit and run, and vehicular homicide—result in lifetime CDL disqualification. For professional drivers who depend on their CDL for their livelihood, aggressive defense of traffic charges is essential.

Points System and License Suspension

Colorado operates a points-based system for traffic violations. Adults who accumulate 12 or more points within any 12-month period, or 18 or more points within any 24-month period, face license suspension. Point values range from 1 point for minor infractions to 12 points for leaving the scene of an accident involving injury. Understanding how points accumulate and taking steps to minimize point impact is an important part of traffic offense defense.

Our attorneys help clients evaluate whether fighting a traffic charge, negotiating a reduced charge, or attending traffic school is the most effective strategy for protecting their driving privileges and avoiding suspension.

Mountain Road and Winter Driving Charges

Western Colorado's mountain environment creates unique traffic enforcement situations. The Colorado Department of Transportation and Colorado State Patrol enforce specific regulations on mountain passes and highways, including chain law requirements during winter months, speed restrictions on steep grades, commercial vehicle regulations on mountain passes, and traction law compliance requirements.

Violations of these mountain-specific regulations can result in traffic citations and, when accidents occur, can serve as evidence of recklessness or carelessness. Our attorneys understand the specific regulations that apply to Western Colorado's mountain roads and defend clients against charges arising from mountain driving situations.
